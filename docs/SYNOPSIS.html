Project synopsis / Estate administration and inheritance tax

The executor's working record

AD Assistant is an open-source web application for executors administering an estate in England and Wales. It carries the whole job in one place: every asset, liability, contact, cost, task and deadline; a live trial balance and per-beneficiary share; a correct, current inheritance tax assessment; the official guidance readable in-app; and drafted paperwork that always waits for a human signature.

It is built for a handful of trusted people, two executors and the family around them, working through the hardest admin most people ever do.

G1

This tool informs and drafts. It is not advice, and nothing is filed automatically.

G2

Agents draft; a person approves; a person submits or sends. No filing, no dispatch, no payment, ever, from code.

G3

All tax and accounting figures come from a deterministic, unit-tested engine. No AI model computes a number.

02 / Guardrails

How it keeps you safe

Four structural decisions, each enforced in code and covered by tests, not stated in a policy document and hoped for.

Deterministic money
Every inheritance tax and estate accounts figure is computed by pure, unit-tested Python with no I/O and no model in the loop: nil rate band, residence nil rate band with the two-million-pound taper, transferred allowances, the excepted-estates test, the charity rate. An executable test table locks the arithmetic; the trial balance carries an always-visible reconciliation check.
backend/app/domain/ · test_iht_engine.py · is_balanced
Draft and approve
Five agent graphs research, explain and draft. None of them has a tool that can send an email, file with HMRC or move money; a guardrail test enumerates every registered tool and proves it. Every draft creates an approval-pending record, and stays a draft until a named person approves it. Submission is always a human act, outside the system.
backend/app/agents/ · test_agent_guardrails.py
Provenance everywhere
Every tax constant and every guidance document carries its source URL and fetch date. Valuations record their source and whether they are estimates or confirmed. The question-answering assistant cites its sources by number and refuses to answer beyond the cached corpus. When a source changes at origin, the change is flagged for human review.
knowledge_doc.source_url · fetch_date · content_hash
Roles enforced server-side
Admin and executors write; viewers are strictly read-only, with executor-private records withheld entirely. Identity comes from a validated Cloudflare Access JWT (signature, audience, issuer, expiry; forged headers rejected; fail closed when unconfigured). An immutable audit trail records every change, approval and sensitive read.

03 / System

A small, well-shaped system

One container, one database, one login layer. Deterministic logic at the core; agents at arm's length, drafting only.

Structural choices worth naming: a single Postgres with pgvector keeps the whole system in one datastore you can back up, restore and take with you; the England-and-Wales rules live behind a jurisdiction interface so the core engine stays regime-agnostic; and the production build is one Docker image serving both the API and the built frontend, with Alembic migrations at start. UK GDPR is taken literally: a complete open-format estate export, and a confirmed, admin-only erase.

05 / Process

The process, worked not read

The administration follows a broadly standard order. AD Assistant seeds it as a 41-step checklist of real tasks with dependencies, so the process is something you work through, not a page you read once. Eight phases, in outline:

Registration and the first days confirm and register the death, order certificates
Verification and the medical certificate; registering within five days; certified copies; the funeral, checking for a prepaid plan and recorded wishes.
Securing and notifying property, Tell Us Once, the notification round
Insuring the empty home and telling the insurer it is unoccupied; the council tax exemption; Tell Us Once within its 28-day window; then the long tail of organisations it does not cover, tracked contact by contact.
Valuing the estate the will, the registers, date-of-death values
Locating the will and confirming the executors; building the asset and liability registers with provenance on every value; estimates replaced by confirmed figures as statements arrive.
Assessing the tax the deterministic engine decides the route
Excepted estate or full IHT400; the schedules checklist derived from what the estate actually contains; payment arranged by the end of the sixth month; a professional-review checkpoint on by default.
Probate submit, wait, receive the grant
The IHT400 submitted by a person; the HMRC code; the probate application and the grant.
Collection and debts collect assets, Section 27 notice, settle creditors
Closing accounts and realising assets; the Gazette and local-paper creditor notice opening its two-month window; the tool blocks safe-to-distribute until the window closes.
Accounts and distribution estate accounts, interim then final distributions
Administration-period tax finalised; the four-account estate accounts approved by the residuary beneficiaries; distributions guarded by the claims window and the executor's year.
Clearance and closure IHT30, record retention
The clearance certificate confirming HMRC has no further claim; records retained for around twelve years, then purged.

Deadlines are never typed in by hand: the statutory dates (IHT payment, IHT400 delivery, Tell Us Once, the creditor and claims windows, the 60-day CGT rule, the loss-relief and deed-of-variation windows) are derived from the date of death and the grant date, and each drives reminders and auto-created tasks.

06 / Boundaries

What it is not

The boundaries are the design. Each of these is deliberate, permanent, and covered by tests where code could cross it.

Not advice
It reports what the official guidance says, with citations, and computes what the published rules imply. It does not, and will not, tell you what you should decide. The Q&A assistant refuses questions its cached sources do not cover rather than improvising.
Not a filing service
Nothing is ever transmitted to HMRC, the probate service, a bank or anyone else. Drafts are prepared and approved inside the tool; submission is a human act outside it. There is no send button, and no code path that could act as one.
Not a replacement for professionals
A near-threshold estate, an unusual will, a trust, a dispute: these are exactly when a solicitor or tax adviser earns their fee. The tool makes their job cheaper by keeping a complete, provenance-tracked record, and it ships with a professional-review checkpoint switched on by default.
Also out of scope by design: jurisdictions beyond England and Wales (extension point provided), will drafting, trust administration, and bank or HMRC API integrations. Manual entry with provenance is the v1 posture.
07 / Open source

Built to be given away

MIT licensed, self-hostable in one command, and structured so the next family, and the next jurisdiction, can use it.

AspectPosition
LicenceMIT. UK Government content in the knowledge library is used under the Open Government Licence with attribution; each installation fetches it from the canonical sources rather than the repository redistributing it.
Your dataThe repository holds schema and synthetic seed only. Real estate data lives in your own self-hosted database, exports as open-format JSON, and can be erased completely. Your data stays yours, and it can leave with you.
Self-hostingcp .env.example .env then docker compose up. A single production image deploys to Railway or anywhere that runs a container. See the installation and deployment guides.
JurisdictionsEngland-and-Wales rules live behind domain/jurisdiction/; the engine is regime-agnostic. Adding Scotland or Northern Ireland means implementing the same interface with its own executable test table, without touching the core.
ContributingGround rules in CONTRIBUTING.md: no personal data ever, deterministic money, tests for domain logic, no send/file/pay tools, UK English, WCAG 2.2 AA. Pull requests that respect the guardrails are welcome.
VersioningSemantic versioning from v0.9.0: a complete, tested tool that has not yet earned its 1.0 stability promises.

The documentation set: a plain-words user guide for executors, an installation guide, a production deployment guide with a tested backup and restore drill, a security policy with an honest known-limits list, and the canonical design masters (architecture, features, data model, agent design, processes).

It informs and drafts. A person decides.
